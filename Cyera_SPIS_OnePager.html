Current State — The Problem Blackstone Has Today
Blackstone has deployed three separate agentic AI systems across its PE portfolio management, investor reporting, and deal sourcing workflows — each accessing unclassified data repositories containing proprietary deal data, LP information, and sensitive financial models. The classification layer is manual, performed quarterly by a two-person data governance team, and covers less than 30% of the cloud data estate. With the board's Q3 mandate to expand AI usage to front-office functions, the exposure window is widening faster than any manual process can close it.
Business Impact — What This Is Costing Them
SEC Reg S-P and proposed data broker rules create direct breach notification risk estimated at $2–8M per incident for a firm managing $1T+ in assets. The current manual classification gap introduces approximately 340 hours per quarter in analyst time that produces incomplete results. More critically, the AI deployment mandate is blocked: the CISO has not approved production expansion until a data governance layer is in place — creating direct board-level tension between the AI growth agenda and the security team's open risk position.
Future State — What Success Looks Like
Complete visibility into all sensitive data across Blackstone's cloud and SaaS estate within 72 hours of Cyera deployment — enabling the CISO to approve AI production expansion to front-office functions before the Q3 board presentation. Automated classification eliminates the 340 analyst hours per quarter and creates a continuous compliance posture for SEC reporting. AI expansion proceeds on the board's timeline with documented data governance controls.
